In physics, one of the most famous equations is Einstein's mass-energy equivalence formula: E = mc2, where E is energy, m is mass, and c is the speed of light. In chemistry, the molecular formula for glucose is C6H12O6, indicating six carbon, twelve hydrogen, and six oxygen atoms. In mathematics, the Pythagorean theorem states that a2 + b2 = c2, where a, b, and c represent the sides of a right-angled triangle. The notation for chemical isotopes can also be written as U235, where the superscript indicates the isotope's mass number. Similarly, subscript is commonly used in formulas like CO2 for carbon dioxide or H2SO4 for sulfuric acid.
