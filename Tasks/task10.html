Main Title (H1)

The H1 heading is used for the most important heading on the page, typically for the title or main topic. It should only appear once per page and is usually the largest heading. It's best suited for page titles, blog post titles, or main headings that represent the overall subject of the page.

Subheading (H2)

The H2 heading is used for major sections within a page. It helps break down content into logical parts under the main heading. Use it for major subtopics, section titles, or important content areas that are a step below the main H1 heading.

Sub-Subheading (H3)

The H3 heading is used for subsections within the H2 sections. It is often used for smaller categories or topics that fall under the larger H2 headings. This is best suited for detailing specific aspects of the sections above.

Smaller Section Title (H4)

The H4 heading is used for even more specific subdivisions of content. It's helpful when you need to divide a topic further within an H3 section. It can be used for subsections, side topics, or further explanations of a concept.

Minor Section Title (H5)

The H5 heading is used for smaller, less important sections. It's rarely used unless your content is heavily structured and needs deeper organization. It can be used for additional notes or small detail sections.

Smallest Section Title (H6)

The H6 heading is the smallest and least prominent heading. It is rarely used but can be helpful for labeling very minor sections, footnotes, or disclaimers. It's suitable for content that is subordinate to all other headings above it.
